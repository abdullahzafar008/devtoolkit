Frequently Asked Questions

What is a polysyllable word? +
Polysyllable words are words that contain three or more syllables (e.g., "representative", "cryptographic"). High polysyllabic density raises readability grade levels.
Why do readability indexes sometimes differ? +
Different indexes use different metrics. For example, the Coleman-Liau formula relies on character count per 100 words, whereas Flesch-Kincaid uses syllables counts.
Is there a limit on text length? +
No. Alphanumeric sets are processed locally within your browser heap, making the analyzer fast even for complete book chapters.
