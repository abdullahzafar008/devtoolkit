Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good Domain Authority (DA) score? +
DA is a logarithmic scale. Scores between 1 and 20 are common for fresh websites. 20 to 50 represents average authority (most small businesses). 50 to 80 is very good (popular blogs, large stores), and 80+ is excellent (Wikipedia, Google).
How do I increase my Domain Authority? +
Focus on building high-quality editorial backlinks from trusted, high-authority domains, writing outstanding unique content, removing broken links, and maintaining a clean spam-free link profile.
Why do `.edu` and `.gov` extensions have higher base trust? +
Academic institution and government websites are strictly regulated and rarely engage in spam linking. Search engines naturally view these extensions as highly authoritative, boosting their domain coefficients.
Does Google use Moz DA for search rankings? +
No. Google does not officially use Moz Domain Authority in its ranking algorithms. Google uses PageRank and hundreds of other proprietary signals, but DA serves as a highly accurate industry proxy.
